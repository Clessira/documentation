title: Activities
description: Create, rename, color, archive — the building blocks of your time tracking.

An **activity** in NowDoing is anything you want to track: `Coding`, `📞 Calls`, `Lunch`, `Reading`. No hierarchy, no projects, no tags — deliberately flat.

## Create an activity

1. Open the popover from the menu bar icon.
2. Click **"Create activity"** (or right-click the icon → **"Create activity"**).
3. Type the name. Emojis are allowed and become part of the name — they show up everywhere.
4. Confirm with <kbd>Enter</kbd>.

**Tip:** a few meaningful activities beat many overly specific ones. Three to eight is a sweet spot. Better one `Coding` activity with notes "Feature X" than ten per-feature activities.

## Manage activities

Right-click the menu bar icon → **Settings → Organize**. From there you can:

- **Rename** — existing entries keep the new name.
- **Color** — used in the status icon, the timeline, and lists.
- **Reorder** — drag and drop. Order drives how activities appear in the popover.
- **Archive** — activity disappears from the quick picker, old entries remain visible.

## Archive, don't delete

NowDoing **does not delete activities** that have entries attached. By design — your historical data should stay consistent.

Instead, **archive** an activity. It leaves the picker but stays visible in lists, and you can un-archive it any time.

## Edit existing entries

In the popover (List view) and in the Timesheet you can adjust individual entries after the fact:

- **Change activity** — if you mis-tapped.
- **Move start or end time** — e.g. if you confirmed the prompt late.
- **Delete entry** — for duplicates or mistakes.

The day total recalculates automatically.
