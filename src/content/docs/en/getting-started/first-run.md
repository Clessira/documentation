## 1. Create activities

NowDoing ships without pre-built categories. You define exactly the activities that fit your day.

1. Click the NowDoing icon in the menu bar.
2. Choose **"Create activity"**.
3. Type a name. Emojis are allowed and become part of the name — e.g. `🛠️ Coding` or `📞 Calls`.

Three to eight activities are usually plenty. You can always add new ones or archive old ones.

## 2. Choose your tracking interval

By default, NowDoing prompts you every **20 minutes**. To adjust:

- Right-click the menu bar icon → **Settings → General**.
- Pick an interval between 5 and 60 minutes.

Tip: shorter intervals (10–15 min) give finer data; longer ones (30–45 min) are less interrupting.

## 3. Answer your first prompt

When your interval elapses, the popover opens automatically with the question **"What did you just work on?"**.

- Click the matching activity — done.
- Or type to filter and press <kbd>Enter</kbd>.

The entry is saved from the previous entry up to now. Gaps (lunch, off-screen time) remain visible.

## 4. Look at the day

A short click on the menu bar icon opens the popover with three views of your day:

- **Groups** — totals per activity.
- **Timeline** — a color bar from morning to evening.
- **List** — chronological, with gaps shown.

For more detail, open the **Timesheet** from the context menu.

## 5. Optional: export

Need the hours for an invoice? The Timesheet has a **CSV export** for day or week — semicolon-separated with a UTF-8 BOM, so Excel and Numbers read German characters correctly.
